Remote Patient Monitoring in Post-Bypass Recovery: A Randomized Trial

Published In JACC: Digital Health
Publication Date October 2021
Primary DOI 10.1016/j.jacadh.2021.08.004
Co-Authors Dr. Ayesha Sharma, David R. Patel

Abstract

"A randomized trial evaluating clinical efficacy and safety of wearable multisensor biosensors combined with cloud-based machine learning analytics to detect early decompensation, acute arrhythmias, and post-operative events in patients recovering at home after Coronary Artery Bypass Graft (CABG) surgery."

Post-operative recovery for bypass patients is traditionally monitored through scheduled office visits. However, complications like atrial fibrillation or fluid retention often peak between appointments, leading to preventable emergency readmissions. This study evaluates how continuous remote biosensor monitoring can help clinical teams intervene early.

Introduction & Digital Framework

After undergoing coronary artery bypass surgery, patients experience a delicate transition when discharged home. Continuous monitoring during this period helps bridge the gap between hospital discharge and recovery, providing reassurance and catching clinical anomalies early.

By using wireless wearable patches that track heart rate, respiratory rate, and fluid indicators, clinical teams can monitor patient recovery in real time and optimize care remotely.

Research Methodology

The randomized, open-label trial evaluated a multi-center post-bypass patient cohort.

  • Participants: 620 post-CABG bypass recovery patients.
  • Group A: Standard clinical post-discharge tracking.
  • Group B: Wearable patch continuous monitor transmitting data to a secure cloud platform.
  • Primary Goal: Comparing 30-day all-cause hospital readmission rates.

Statistical Significance Value (p-value)

Continuous monitoring significantly reduced emergency readmission rates. Most anomalies were managed successfully at home through remote medication adjustments and clinical consultations.

Conclusion & Practical Impact

Continuous remote monitoring for post-bypass patients reduces 30-day readmissions and improves care delivery. By catching complications early, digital health solutions help make post-operative recovery safer and more efficient.
