Stress Echocardiography vs CT Angiography in Stable Coronary Disease

Published In European Heart Journal
Publication Date April 2020
Primary DOI 10.1093/eurheartj/ehz994
Co-Authors Dr. Ayesha Sharma, Sung-Min Lee

Abstract

"A prospective head-to-head clinical trial comparing diagnostic accuracy, downstream referral for invasive angiography, total cost of care, and 3-year clinical outcomes in symptomatic patients referred for stress echocardiography versus computed tomography angiography (CTA)."

Stable coronary artery disease requires accurate, timely diagnosis to plan effective treatment. Both stress echocardiography (evaluating wall motion during exercise/stress) and coronary CT angiography (directly visualizing coronary anatomy) are widely used. This study compares their diagnostic and economic outcomes head-to-head.

Introduction & Clinical Comparison

Stress echocardiography evaluates functional ischemia (heart muscle strain under stress), whereas CT angiography (CTA) is a structural test that directly visualizes plaque buildup. Selecting the optimal test impacts downstream diagnostic steps and total healthcare costs.

Our research evaluated a large symptomatic cohort to help clinical teams select the most appropriate diagnostic path for suspected stable coronary disease.

Research Methodology

The trial utilized a prospective, randomized diagnostic registry across three heart imaging academies.

  • Patient Database: 1,400 symptomatic outpatient subjects with suspected coronary disease.
  • Arm A: Stress echocardiography (exercise or pharmacological).
  • Arm B: Multi-detector Computed Tomography Angiography (CTA).
  • Follow-up Strategy: Referral rates for invasive coronary angiograms and 3-year cardiac event tracking.

While CT angiography showed slightly higher anatomical diagnostic accuracy, stress echocardiography was more cost-effective and resulted in fewer unnecessary invasive angiograms for non-obstructive disease.

Conclusion & Clinical Recommendations

Both tests are valuable diagnostic tools. CT angiography offers excellent structural visualization to rule out disease early, while stress echocardiography provides functional insight to guide cost-effective clinical management and reduce unnecessary invasive procedures.
