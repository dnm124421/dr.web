Statin Efficacy & Dosage Optimization in South Asian Populations

Published In BMJ (British Medical Journal)
Publication Date September 2019
Primary DOI 10.1136/bmj.l4215
Co-Authors Dr. Ayesha Sharma, Vijay Krishnan

Abstract

"A multi-center cohort study evaluating lipid-lowering efficacy, patient tolerability, dosage thresholds, and adverse event profiles of high-intensity statin therapy in South Asian individuals presenting with high cardiovascular risk scores."

South Asian populations have a significantly higher risk of premature coronary artery disease. However, standard global guidelines for statin therapy are largely based on Western clinical data. This study addresses this gap by investigating efficacy, dosage optimization, and tolerability in South Asian cohorts.

Introduction & Clinical Context

Premature coronary artery disease in South Asian individuals is often characterized by aggressive plaque buildup, elevated lipoprotein(a) levels, and unique metabolic profiles. Despite high cardiovascular risk, clinicians sometimes hesitate to prescribe high-intensity statin therapy due to concerns about muscle pain or liver enzyme elevations.

This study evaluates these safety concerns and provides evidence-based dosing recommendations for this high-risk population.

Research Methodology

The prospective registry-based study followed a diverse cohort over several years.

  • Patient Database: 3,200 high-cardiovascular-risk South Asian participants.
  • Therapeutic Agents: Standard daily doses of Atorvastatin or Rosuvastatin.
  • Parameters Evaluated: Baseline and interval lipid profiles, liver enzymes, and muscle symptom tracking.

The trial confirmed that lower doses of high-intensity statins (such as 15-20mg Rosuvastatin) achieved target LDL reduction with excellent tolerability and minimal side effects in South Asian cohorts.

Discussion & Preventive Application

South Asian patients show excellent lipid-lowering response to moderate-to-high intensity statins. Prescribing optimized dosages achieves target cholesterol reduction safely with low side effects, supporting proactive preventive cardiology interventions.
